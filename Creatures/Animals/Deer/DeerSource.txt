### Deer Tactics
Most deer, from tiny pudus to supernatural caribou, generally don't seek out combat as they are herbivores. If there is some supernatural compulsion driving them to attack, they will utilize hit-and-run tactics. They charge in with their horns before running out of reach to repeat the process on their next turn, using their Back Kick against anyone who makes an opportunity attack against them.

#### Ice Caribou
These creatures will focus on bringing down anyone capable of dealing fire damage. Their ice attacks help enable their hit-and-run tactics by slowing down their targets.

### Deer Loot
The valuable items looted from deer will generally come from whatever can be harvested from them. This harvesting can usually be done using a **Wisdom (Survival)** or **Intelligence (Nature)** check. However, you may allow players proficient with specific tools to harvest items related to that tool. For example, a character might make a Wisdom (Leathworkerâ€™s Tools) check to harvest hide.

Most of an ice caribou's form will melt away when it is destroyed. Players can loot the insolvable bits of ice and melt water it leaves behind to find objects still holding some vestiges of its power. This can be done with an **Intelligence (Nature)** or **Wisdom (Nature)** check.

Alternatively, you can roll for the loot randomly using a flat modifier.
