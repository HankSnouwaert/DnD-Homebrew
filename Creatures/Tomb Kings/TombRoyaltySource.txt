> ## Tomb Prince
>*Medium undead, unaligned*
> ___
> - **Armor Class** 16 (breastplate and Eternal Empire)
> - **Hit Points** 114 (12d8 + 60)
> - **Speed** 30 ft.
>___
>|STR|DEX|CON|INT|WIS|CHA|
>|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
>|18 (+4)|14 (+2)|20 (+5)|12 (+1)|14 (+2)|18 (+4)|
>___
> - **Saving Throws** +7 to all saves with My Will Be Done
> - **Skills** Athletics +7, History +5, Religion +5, Persuasion +7
> - **Damage Vulnerabilities** fire
> - **Damage resistance** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks
> - **Damage Immunities** necrotic, poison
> - **Condition Immunities** charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned
> - **Senses** darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 12
> - **Languages** The languages it knew in life
> - **Challenge** 6 (2,300 XP)   &emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;  **Proficiency Bonus** +3      
> ___
>
> ***Eternal Empire.*** While the tomb prince is not incapacitated, it and any other undead creature within 30 feet of it can choose to make its AC equal to 10 + the Tomb Prince’s Intelligence modifier + the Tomb Prince’s Charisma modifier (AC 16).
>
> ***Marshal Undead.*** Unless the tomb prince is incapacitated, it and undead creatures of its choice within 60 feet of it have advantage on saving throws against features that turn undead.
>
> ***My Will Be Done.*** While the tomb prince is not incapacitated, whenever it or any other undead creature within 30 feet of it makes an attack or saving throw, that creature can use the tomb prince’s Charisma modifier + its Proficiency Bonus (+7) on the roll the rather use its own modifier.
> 
> ***Strike of Terror.*** Once per turn, when the tomb prince hits a creature with its khopesh, it may force that creature to make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature falls prone and is frightened of the tomb prince until the end of the tomb prince's next turn or until the tomb prince is incapacitated. While frightened this way, the target can't stand up.
>
> ***Vengeful Curse*** A creature that kills the tomb prince must make a DC 15 Charisma saving throw or become cursed. While cursed this way, the target automatically fails any death saving throws it makes and treats the roll as if it rolled a 1.
>
> ### Action
>
> ***Multiattack.*** The tomb prince makes two attacks. 
>
> ***Khopesh.*** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 7 (1d6 + 4) slashing damage plus 10 (3d6) psychic damage.

Curse of the gods

Many of the Tomb King have a patron god that hold above all others, these gods enacted divine punishment it their unique way so that transgressors knows whom they angered. You may choose to one of the fallowing curse to placed on creature when a creature kills a tomb king or prince.

### Cruse of the Deaths Grip.
Most common amongst the those that have under gone mummification are gods of the funerary cults that create them, those that have their curse shall feel the pull grave being much stronger as death approaches.

While Cursed, the target automatically fails any death saving throws it makes and treats the roll as if it rolled a 1.

### Cruse of the Blazing Sun.
Many of the greatest kings of old claimed to be blessed or even descends of gods of the sun, any who would cut down one of noble blood will have the sun as their enemy. 

While the cursed creature is in sunlight, it has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

### Cruse of the Marked Prey.
Fierce hunters and warriors were chosen by great conquers to aid them on their campaigns, One who slays a favored warrior of such a god will become and easy targets.

Weapon attacks rolls against the cursed creature can't gain disadvantage.

### Cruse of the Lost Lessons.
The great gods of knowledge have many rules preying for them to depart their wisdom, while the ones that cut down their pupils will striped what they have learned.

The cursed creature can't add it's proficiency to skill checks, tool checks, and saving throws.

### Cruse of the Serpents Kiss.
The snakes honored and feared in many ancient kingdoms, many vile rules call upon these gods for detective powers, should a mortal cross such a god, they will feel their venom with every cut. 

The creature loses immunity and resistance to poison damage, also if the creature takes any slashing, prince or poison damage, it deals additional 2 (1d4) poison damage to the creature. If the cruse was from a tomb king it is 4 (1d8)



The Tomb king may have different secondary effect for it's hit better fit it's god, The DC For a Tomb Prince is DC 15, A Tomb King is 17

### Venom Strike

 The additional damage is poison damage
 
 Once per turn when the Tomb King hit's a creature, it may have it make a constitution saving throw or be poisoned  end of the Tomb Prince's next turn, while it is poisoned, if it fails a saving throw and becomes poisoned by another effect, it is stunned until the end of it's next turn.
 
### Sun Strike

The additional damage is radiant damage

Once per turn when the Tomb King hit's a creature, it may have it make a constitution saving throw or blinded until the end of the Tomb Prince's next turn

### Siphoning Strike

The additional damage is necrotic damage

Once per turn when the Tomb King hit's a creature, it may have it make a constitution saving throw or gain a level of exhaustion and the tomb king gains advantage on attack rolls and saving throws until the end of the Tomb Kings next turn.

### Strike of Rulership

The additional damage is psychic damage

 it may have it make a Wisdom saving throw or fall prone and be frightened of the Tomb Prince until the end of the Tomb Prince's next turn or until the Tomb Prince is incapacitated, while frightened this way the target can't stand up.
 
### Strike of Nulling

The additional damage is force damage

 it may have it make a Strength saving throw or drops a object of the Tomb Prince's choice and can have the object flung up to 10 feet away in direction of it's choice., if the object is magical, the Properties and Powers of magic item are suppressed for the next minute.
