## Savage Idols
*Rampaging Constructs Animated By Orcish Faith*<br>
**Habitat:** Desert, Grasslands, Hills, Mountains, Underdark; **Treasure:** Relics

Orcs are typically warmongers, ever eager to leap from one blood-soaked conflict to the next while shirking anything that resembles sustained labor. Consequently, they rarely construct lasting structures or leave anything permanent behind, even in the places where they pause briefly.
Yet, on rare occasions following a particularly momentous victory, an orc shaman may experience a divine revelation. Seized by this vision, the shaman compels their reluctant comrades to gather stones, broken metal scraps, and shattered wood from the wreckage of their conquests. These materials are heaped into a rough effigy of an Orcish deity, daubed with primitive markings that imbue it with sacred power. If the tribe’s fervor is strong enough, that combined surge of aggression can rouse the statue to life, making it a living embodiment of Orcish wrath.

Such monstrosities are known as savage idols, and they rank among the most devastating creations of orcish magic. No two idols look exactly alike, but they commonly rise to around sixteen feet in height, fashioned primarily from stone with scattered bits of metal or wood interwoven throughout. Threads of green magic pulse through their frames, binding them together as they lumber forth in an unrelenting quest to devastate anything in their path.

For any foe unfortunate enough to attract its gaze, a savage idol is akin to a natural calamity. Its massive limbs can smash fully armored warriors into pulp, while its rocky hide shrugs off most counterattacks. 

\columnbreak

<br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br>

Even more terrifying is the morale boost it offers to nearby orcs, who channel the idol’s sheer ferocity into a frenzy of violence. Facing even a single orc inspired by such a hulking symbol of might is a challenge few warriors survive.

Despite its awe-inspiring power, a savage idol suffers from crucial flaws. Its drive to destroy is absolute, leaving no room for strategy or self-preservation. Worse yet, its impatience for battle knows no bounds; if enemies do not appear swiftly enough, the idol may turn on its own warband in frustration. Moreover, its continued existence hinges on a constant wellspring of orcish fury. Without that energy, it gradually grows dormant, reverting to an inert heap of stone and scrap. However, the enchantments within it never fully fade. Years—or even decades—can pass before a fresh wave of orcish magic or a new horde of marauding greenskins stirs the slumbering idol to rampage once again.

In rare cases, savage idols have fallen under the sway of forces other than orcs. Particularly cunning or powerful spellcasters might revive an ancient idol’s remains or even wrest limited control of a newly awakened one from an orcish warband. But no matter how strong the magic, they can only guide—not command—this living engine of ruin, which ultimately remains loyal to the roar of war above all else.

### Savage Idol Tactics
Idols employ simple tactics, aiming to hit as many enemies as possible with melee attacks. If they are unable to do so, they resort to a rock attack.

### The Big 'Un
The Big’n stands as a legend among all Orcish kind—an idol of such colossal size and power that no other effigy has ever equaled its holy ferocity. Many orcs believe it to be the embodiment of their god walking the earth. Over the centuries, mighty war tribes have added their own sacred touches to its form, layering on enchanted totems, rune stones, and even the remnants of other savage idols. With each added artifact, the Big 'Un has expanded to awe-inspiring proportions.

On the rare occasions when the Big 'Un awakens, entire landscapes are reshaped, and settlements vanish beneath its unstoppable fury. Massive hordes of zealous orcs follow close behind, chanting praises to what they view as the living embodiment of their divine patron. Its might is so overwhelming that if any portion of the idol is torn free—whether by a lucky blow or immensely powerful attack—it comes alive on its own and continues fighting. However, these rampages are short-lived: the unrelenting violence required to keep the Big 'Un on its feet is too great to sustain for long.

In the centuries since its first recorded appearance, countless conflicts have ignited over the remains of the Big 'Un. Knightly orders and other warriors of the civilized world seek to secure its fragments, preventing them from falling back into orcish hands. 

\columnbreak

<br>

Unable to destroy the idol’s sacred pieces, they instead scatter and hide them across distant lands. Undeterred, orcish warbands embark on sacred quests to reclaim and reassemble these shards. Many tribes prophesize that one day, a warlord with a horde vast enough will rise, awakening the Big 'Un for good—never allowing it to fall silent again.

#### Destroying the Big 'Un and Its Shards

If you wish to incorporate the Big’n into your campaign, here are several ways it might be destroyed:

**Rivalry Among Orcs.** If two orc war bosses battle over a fragment of the Big 'Un, their struggle may shatter it.

**Peaceborne Annihilation.** If a peace treaty between two warring nations is signed upon the remains of the Big 'Un, the idol's power could be nullified.

**The Cry of Fear.** If a shaman or warlord standing near the remains of the Big 'Un lets out a scream of pure terror, its essence may dissipate.

**Sacred Ruination.** To ensure the Big 'Un’s final destruction, each of its four remaining shards must be inscribed with a master rune, each carved by a different rune-smith. Once completed, the shards must then be placed atop separate mountain peaks, where they will be destroyed.
