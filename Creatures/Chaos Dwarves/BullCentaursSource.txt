## Bull Centaurs
*Cruel Half-Bull Half-Dwarf Amalgams*<br>
**Habitat:** Desert, Grasslands, Mountain, Underdark, Urban, Volcanic; **Treasure:** Armaments <br>

Long ago, when hellish fiends first corrupted the forefathers of the fel dwarfs, they led them down a path of damnation. Some dwarfs, so thoroughly inundated with this insidious essence, had their resilient bodies and spirits utterly crushed. This unholy power fused their broken forms with savage aurochs, birthing the monstrous beings known as bull centaurs.

These nightmares possess the lower body of a muscular bovine and the upper half of a beastly dwarf, complete with large, curved horns jutting from their skulls. They embody the stubbornness and resilience of both bloodlines, combined with a fanatical devotion to the dark entities that fashioned them. Once roused to anger, bull centaurs are nearly unstoppableâ€”hurtling forward with savage momentum, they slam into foes with horns, axes, and hooves, gouging, chopping, and pulverizing flesh.

Despite their massive bulk, bull centaurs strike with brutal precision. They often leave victims barely clinging to life, either to be devoured alive in ritual feasts or dragged to gore-soaked altars to be torn apart in sacrifice.
While some small tribes of bull centaurs roam the wastelands, butchering any they come across as offerings to their dark gods, most have been conscripted into the empires of the fel dwarfs. There, they serve the demon-smith prophets as elite guardians of the most malignant shrines and ziggurats of the dark gods. When the fel dwarfs march to war in great numbers, herds of bull centaurs often stampede at the vanguard, acting as terrifying shock cavalry.

Since their first emergence, only a handful of bull centaurs are born in each generation of fel dwarfs. These rare children are taken from birth and raised by the demon-smith prophets. Yet the limited number of these elite soldiers cannot satisfy the ambitions of their cruel masters.

\columnbreak

<br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br>

In pursuit of greater power, the demon-smiths employ their mastery of forbidden arts, seeking to twist their own children into more bull centaurs to swell their ranks. Most of these unnatural experiments end in agonizing death. Others are kept alive only through accursed concoctions, regularly injected to prolong their misbegotten lives, or through infernal prosthetics grafted onto their malformed, failing bodies.

<br>

> ##### New Condition: Resolute
> The creature stubbornly digs in to weather any assault. It experiences the following effects:
> * Attack rolls made against the creature have disadvantage.
> * The creature can't take its move action on its turn.
> * The creature makes Strength checks and all saving throws with advantage, except Dexterity saving throws, which it makes with disadvantage.
The Resolute condition ends if an effect moves the creature against its will, knocks it prone, or the creature becomes charmed, frightened, or incapacitated. A creature that is prone, charmed, frightened, or incapacitated can't become Resolute.

### Bull Centaur Warriors
Armed with massive axes and heavy shields, the rank of Warrior is the most common among their kind. Patrols of these Bull Centaurs can often be found guarding temples within the obsidian cities of the fel dwarves.

\columnbreak



### Bull Centaur Renders

More unruly than their brethren, bull centaur renders wield brutal twin axes. They hurl these weapons with ease at fleeing foes who manage to evade their crushing hooves.

### Bull Centaur Khans
Those bull centaurs who possess the greatest cunning and might among their kind rise to become khansâ€”also known as bull lords. These formidable specimens often lead herds of their kin, whether roaming the desolate wilderness or marching within the armies of the fel dwarves. At times, a khan serves directly under a powerful dwarven practitioner, acting as a trusted vassal and executing their master's will without question.

### Bull Centaur Tactics
#### Bull Centaur Warriors
If the warrior has a clear, straight path to an enemy, it will use its Resolute Charge. If no such path is available, it will stand its ground and fight enemies already in melee range (still using Resolute Charge without moving to gain the Resolute condition).
 
#### Bull Centaur Render
Renders are more adaptable than their kin. If denied a clear charge lane or if enemies keep disengaging, they will stand their ground and throw their twin axes at ranged targets.


#### Bull Centaur Guardian
Guardians typically operate in one of two roles:

**Personal Bodyguard:** Often assigned to protect a powerful spellcaster such as a deamonsmith, the guardian stays close to its charge to grant it its defensive aura. It may reposition itself with a charge if necessary to eliminate isolated threats.


**Group Anchor:** When guarding a formation of other bull centaurs, the guardian charges alongside them to ensure the group benefits from its defensive aura. The group holds formation unless disrupted by sustained area-of-effect attacks.


#### Bull Centaur Ravager
 Ravagers follow similar tactics to renders but prioritize attacking any enemy suffering from the Frightened condition.

#### Bull Centaur Khan
The khan opens combat with Stampede to shatter enemy front lines. Afterwards, it uses tactics similar to standard bull centaur warriors.

#### Optional: Magic Loot for Item Crafting
If you plan to use harvested loot for crafting magic items, you can try to thematically align the effects of the magic items being crafted with the creature's they're harvested from. In the case of bull centaurs, Fiendish effects would make the most sense. These could be magic item effects that steal the lives of their victims or conjure unholy magic.

***Magic Loot GP Values.*** The value of magic ingredients depends on your crafting system. Subtract their cost from the normal lootâ€™s gp value. If the ingredient is worth significantly more, subtract it from the next treasure hoard instead.

> ##### New Condition: Resolute
> The creature stubbornly digs in to weather any assault. It experiences the following effects:
> * Attack rolls made against the creature have disadvantage.
> * The creature can't take its move action on its turn.
> * The creature makes Strength checks and all saving throws with advantage, except Dexterity saving throws, which it makes with disadvantage.
The Resolute condition ends if an effect moves the creature against its will, knocks it prone, or the creature becomes charmed, frightened, or incapacitated. A creature that is prone, charmed, frightened, or incapacitated can't become Resolute.
