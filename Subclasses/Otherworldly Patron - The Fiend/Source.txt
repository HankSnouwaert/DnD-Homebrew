## Otherworldly Patron

### The Fiend patron
You have made a pact with a fiend from the lower planes of existence, a being whose aims are evil, even if you strive against those aims. Such beings desire the corruption or destruction of all things, ultimately including you. Fiends powerful enough to forge a pact include demon lords such as Demogorgon, Orcus, Fraz'Urb-luu, and Baphomet; archdevils such as Asmodeus, Dispater, Mephistopheles, and Belial; pit fiends and balors that are especially mighty; and ultroloths and other lords of the yugoloths.

#### Blood Gift
Starting at 1st level, when a hostile creature dies with in 30 feet of you with a challenge ratting equal to or lower then your warlock level + your charisma modifier (minimum of 1), you can regain a number of hit points equal to the creatures challenge ratting (round up for 1/8 1/4 and 1/2)
You can use this feature only if you aren't incapacitated. 

#### Dark Bargain 

Starting at 6th level, you can call on your patron to alter fate in your favor, for a price. When you fails a saving throw or ability check, you add a d12 to the roll, potentially turning the save into a success. You loses hit points equal to the number roll on the d12.
Once you succeed on, an ability check, or a saving throw because of this feature, you canâ€™t use the feature again until you finish a short or long rest.

#### Fiendish Resilience
Starting at 10th level, you can choose one damage type when you finish a short or long rest. You gain resistance to that damage type until you choose a different one with this feature. Damage from magical weapons or silver weapons ignores this resistance.

#### Hurl Through Hell
At 14th level, when you hit a creature with an attack or a creature fails a saving throw against your spell, you can use this feature to instantly transport the target through the lower planes. The creature disappears and hurtles through a nightmare landscape.
At the end of your next turn, the target returns to the space it previously occupied, or the nearest unoccupied space. If the target is not a fiend, it takes 10d10 psychic damage as it reels from its horrific experience.

#### Old One Eldritch Invocations.

#### Executioner's Blade
Prerequisite: The Fiend patron, Pact of the Blade feature.

When you create your pact weapon melee attack you choose to have it be made of twisted and chard demon bones that hunger for death.

When reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a challenge ratting equal to or lower then your warlock level + your charisma modifier (minimum of 1), with this weapon, you gain temporary hit points equal to it's challenge ratting.

#### Blood Script

Prerequisite: The Fiend patron patron, Pact of the tome feature.

A new page appears in your Book of Shadows, As part of a short rest you may spend 10 minutes writing the name of a warlock spell you know of 5 level or lower on the page in your own blood, at the end of the short rest you lose Xd12 life were X is the spells level.
While the book is on your person you can choose to cast the spell write on the page with out expending a spell slot, when you do the writing disappears.

The writing disappears after 8 hours or when you wright another spell on the page

#### Pilfering Fiend

Prerequisite: The Fiend patron, Pact of the chain feature.

When you regain hit points form your Blood Gift feature, you may have your familiar gain that many temporary hit points as well.

#### Morbid Talisman
Prerequisite: The The Fiend patron, Pact of the Talisman feature
When you would regain hit points form your Blood Gift feature, you can have the wearer of your talisman gains the hit points instead.
