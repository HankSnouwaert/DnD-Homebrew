## Otherworldly Patron

### The undead patron
You have made a pact with a fiend from the lower planes of existence, a being whose aims are evil, even if you strive against those aims. Such beings desire the corruption or destruction of all things, ultimately including you. Fiends powerful enough to forge a pact include demon lords such as Demogorgon, Orcus, Fraz'Urb-luu, and Baphomet; archdevils such as Asmodeus, Dispater, Mephistopheles, and Belial; pit fiends and balors that are especially mighty; and ultroloths and other lords of the yugoloths.

#### Undying From

Starting at 1st level, you manifest learn the frist secrets to undeath. When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, activate can special from, while in this form you the fallowing benefits.

*	Your creature type becomes undead
*	You a ward that has hit points equal to four times your warlock level + your proficiency bonus. Whenever you take damage, the pool takes the damage instead. If this damage reduces the ward to 0 hit points, you take any remaining damage. While you have energy in this pool, having 0 hit points doesn’t knock you unconscious. and you don't make death saving throw, and you don't suffer the normal effects of taking damage while at 0 hit points.

This form ends onces you have 1 or more hit points or your pool has no more hit points. 
Your ward regains it's hitpoints once you finish a long rest 

#### Hex Stalker
Starting at 6th level, You know how to pull creature form the shadow fell to hunt down those you have cursed: you know the Summon Shadowspawn spell. It doesn't count against the number of Warlock spells you know, if you already know the spell you may learn a another warlock spell instead.

You can cast Summon Shadowspawn without a material components. You can also cast it once without using a spell slot, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. when you cast it this way, Shadowspawn gains temporary hit points equal to your warlock level.

Whenever you start casting the spell while a creature is under your Hex Weaver's Curse, you can modify it so that it doesn't require concentration. If you do so, the Shadow Spirit created by the spell can only attack the cursed creature and spell ends when the Curse ends.


#### Armor of Hexes
At 10th level, your hex grows more powerful. If the target cursed by your Hex Weaver's Curse hits you with an attack roll, you can use your reaction to roll a d6. On a 4 or higher, the attack instead misses you, regardless of its roll.

#### Master of Hexes
Starting at 14th level, you can spread your Hex Weaver's Curse from a slain creature to another creature. When the creature cursed by your Hex Weaver's Curse dies, you can apply the curse to a different creature you can see within 30 feet of you, provided you aren't incapacitated. When you apply the curse in this way, you don't regain hit points from the death of the previously cursed creature.

#### The Hex Weaver Invocations.

#### Hexblade
Prerequisite: The Hex Weaver patron, Pact of the Blade feature.

You can	 create a Melee Weapon forged from silver, with black runes etched into it, using your Pact of the Blade feature. You can use your Charisma modifier, instead of Strength or Dexterity, for the attack and damage rolls with this weapon.

If you reduce a target cursed by your Hexblade’s Curse to 0 hit points with this Weapon, you may trap some of it's life essence in the Weapon,  When you take the attack action, you can forgo one of your attacks with the weapon to make a  ranged spell attack against a target you can see with in 120 feet of you. On a hit, the target takes necrotic damage equal to 2d10 + your Charisma modifier.

The weapon can hold a number of essences equal to your Charisma modifier at a time, essences dispate after 8 hours.


#### Ward of Hexes
Prerequisite: The Hex Weaver patron, Pact of the Talisman feature

When the wearer of your talisman is hit by an attacker you can see within 30 feet of you and that is cursed by your Hex Weaver's Curse, you can use your reaction to roll a d6. On a 4 or higher, the attack instead misses, regardless of its roll. 

You can use this feature can’t use a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and all expended uses are restored when you finish a long rest.


#### Vengeful Hex

Prerequisite: The Hex Weaver patron, Pact of the tome feature.

A new page appears in your Book of Shadows. you can spend 10 minutes drawing a perfect image of a specific creature that you have seen clearly before. you can only have one image of a creature at a time on the page. You can magically erase a picture on the page by touching it.

With your Book of Shadows in hand, you can use your bonus action to use your Hex Weaver's Curse  on a creature who image is on the page with out expending a use of your Hex Weaver's  feature.

Once you curse a creature this way you can't do so until you finish a long rest


#### Hex Parasite
Prerequisite: The Hex Weaver patron, Pact of the chain feature.
When you use you bonus action to use Hex Weaver's Curse, you can command your familiar to take the Attack action as part of the same bonus action.

Your familiar  also gains the following benefits while a creature is cursed.
⦁	Whenever your familiar hits a creature cursed by your Hex Weaver's Curse with an attack roll, it deals an extra 5 damage to the target.

⦁	Any attack roll your familiar makes against the cursed target is a critical hit on a roll of 19 or 20 on the d20.

⦁	Attack rolls the cursed target makes against you familiar can't gain advantage.
