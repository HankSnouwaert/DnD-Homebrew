## Bard College

### College of Choir
Worship takes on many forms across the multiverse; the quiet contemplation of the monastic hermit, the insightful guidance of the temple priest, the resolute dedication of the faith-filled zealot, and the impassioned preacher spreading the tenants of their faith across the land with word and song.

Choir bards are devout preachers of the divine, and all their performances sing the praise of their deity. For good or ill, these bards use the power of their voice to further the will of their god, and inspire devotion in their mortal followers

#### Divine Gospel
At 3rd level, whenever you would make a Intelligence (religion) check, you can make a Charisma (religion) check instead. You can also make a Charisma (religion) check in place of Charisma (Persuasion), Charisma (Intimidation), Charisma (performance) check you make when you are attempting to convert others to warship your god or spreading your religion.

#### Divine inspiration.
At 3rd level, You call onto god to inspire. When creature you can see or hear rolls a bardic dice, you may roll 20. If you roll a number equal to or lower than your your Charisma modifier + your proficiency bonus, your deity intervenes and the creature can choose to treat the bardic dice as though it was the maximum number rather then roll it. Once you use this feature, you can't use this feature again until you finish a short or long rest.


#### Inspire the Masses
Starting at 6th level, You can expend a use of bardic inspiration a bonus action on your turn to choose up to three creatures other then yourself within 60 feet of you who can hear you. Thouse creatures each gain a d4 Bardic Inspiration die.

In addition when you apply your Divine inspiration to a d4 Bardic Inspiration, The creature rolls your normal Bardic Inspiration die instead of a d4.

When you reach certain 10th level in this class, you give up to four creatures d4 Bardic Inspiration die, and up to five creatures at 15th level.

#### Healing Feath
Starting at 14th level, When a creature adds your bardic inspiration to a roll, the creatures gain life equal to the result of the bardic inspiration.

#### Blessed Potency
Starting at 6th level, you others that follow are blessed with divine might in battle. When a creature takes damage from one of your cantrips or weapon attacks, you can also deal additional damage equal to one roll of your bardic inspiration die to that creature. Once you deal this damage, you can't use this feature again until the start of your next turn. The type of damage is determined by the Divine Domain you choose.

In addition whenever you cast bard spell with casting time of one action and a verbal component, you can choose to have a creature targeted buy the spell deal additional damage equal to one roll of your bardic inspiration die to that creature the next time it deals damage with one of it's cantrips or weapon attacks before the end of it's next turn.
