## Non-Stat Block Example Content

#### Lair Action
When Khalindra rolls initiative, her lair awakens to aid her, and until combat ends, the lair has an Intelligence 18 and can act of its own accord but always acts in Khalindra (of the Avatar of The Asp's) best interest. While active this way, the lair is aware of the location of any creatures within it. On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the lair can cause an Archer of the Asp to appear in an unoccupied space within 30 feet of the Khalindra or the Avatar of the Asp, which remain for one minute or until destroyed. Archers summoned this way act on initiative count 0 of each round. The if lair attempts to summon a skeletion whilte their are already three or more active Archers, It must choose for one to be immediately destroyed.

#### Regional Effects
The region surrounding Khalindra's lair is affected by her presence, creating one or more of the following effects:

• **Poisoned Waters.** Small bodies of water within 5 miles of the lair become poisonous. A creature that drinks this water must succeed on a DC 19 Constitution saving throw or become Poisoned for 8 hours. An affected creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each hour, ending the effect on a success.

• **Hate of the Dead.** Any Undead within 5 miles of the layer lose immunity to the Poisoned condition and immunity and resistance to Poison damage. Khalindra can choose for a undead creatrue not to be effect.

• **Living Statues.** Serpentine engravings and statues within 1 mile constantly writhe and hiss at those who walk in their shadow. These magical objects have an Intelligence of 8, a Wisdom of 16, and a Charisma of 14. They possess hearing and blindsight out to a range of 30 feet.
They can speak and understand Common and communicate telepathically with Khalindra.

While in her lair, Khalindra's challenge rating increases by 3.

### Epic Battle: Form of The Asp

You may choose to have an Avatar of The Asp (an inert magical object) reside within Khalindra’s lair. While inert, it has resistance to all damage and immunity to any magical effects, while retaining its hit points and AC.
When Khalindra dies, you may have her spirit rise from her body and possess the statue. The Avatar of the Asp immediately becomes active and regains all of its hit points, falling under Khalindra’s full control. Her initiative count remains unchanged. 

The avatar may perform an 8-hour ritual in her lair, returning Khalindra to her original body, fully restoring her, or providing a new body if the original no longer exists. Once the ritual is complete, the Avatar of the Asp becomes inert again.

When she has access to this ability, both her forms combined are treated as a CR 23 creature.

### Alternate Weapon: Volley Gun

You can choose to replace the Steam Cannon action station with the following action station:

> ***Volley Gun (1 crew, three-quarters cover).*** The user spends up to 6 Steam Points and picks a target it can see within 600 feet to fire at. The target must make a *Dexterity Saving Throw:* DC 14 (or 15 for the Emperor's Wrath) + the number of Steam Points spent. <br>
> *Failure:* The creature takes 10 (3d6) Piercing damage for each Steam Point spent. <br>
> *Success:* Half damage.

### Monstrous Actions

If you wish to make your vampire a true solo threat, you may grant it an extra Monstrous Action it can use each round of combat, increasing its CR by 1.

This grants the vampire an additional action in combat, taken on Initiative Count 21 (losing ties), separate from the vampire’s normal initiative. On that count, the vampire can take one of the following actions, provided it is not Incapacitated; it may still use Tenacious Will even while Incapacitated.

**Tenacious Will.**  If the vampire is subject to an ongoing effect that allows a saving throw at the start or end of its turn (or as an action), it can immediately make that saving throw with advantage. On a success, the effect ends.

**Rush.**  The vampire moves up to 20 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

**Swipe.**  *Melee Weapon Attack:* Vampiric Power Bonus to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 19 (3d12) Slashing damage.

**Vampiric Power.** The vampire uses one of its Vampiric Powers.

**Spellcasting.** The vampire casts a 1st- or 2nd-level spell it knows from any spellcasting feature it has.

You may choose to grant the vampire additional Monstrous Actions on Initiative Count 15 and Initiative Count 0 (losing ties). For each additional action granted this way, the vampire’s challenge rating increases by 1.

###  Vampiric Traits & Powers

Any vampire can be given any of the following Vampiric Traits or Vampiric Powers. Vampiric Traits are passive abilities, and each one granted increases its CR by 1 unless otherwise stated. Vampric Powers can be used as a Bonus Action, and granting one or more of these powers increases the vampire's CR by 1. If a Vampiric Power involves an attack roll or forces a saving throw, the attack bonus or save DC used can be found in the Vampire's stat block in the Vampiric Power bonus action. 

\columnbreak

#### Flying Horror (Vampiric Trait)

*Some vampires can rise into the night sky, floating effortlessly and soaring through the heavens. From above, they swoop down to gorge themselves on prey below.*

The Vampire has a Fly Speed of 60 feet and can hover. Vampire Masters and Vampire Lords gain a fly speed of 70.

#### Misty Step (Vampiric Power)

*When threatened, the vampire can dissolve into vapor and vanish, only to reform elsewhere moments later.*

The vampire teleports up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space that it can see.

#### Regeneration (Vampiric Trait)

*Even when inflicted with wounds that would kill any mortal, the vampire’s body continually renews itself. Torn flesh knits back together in moments, and shattered bones fuse and become whole once more.*

The vampire regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn if it has at least 1 hit point and isn't in sunlight or running water. If the vampire takes Radiant damage or damage from holy water, this trait doesn't function at the start of the vampire's next turn.

#### Spectral Form (Vampiric Trait)

*The most formidable vampires have shed their physical bodies, becoming phantasmal beings able to pass effortlessly through walls, barriers, and fortifications. Conventional weapons are largely ineffective against these specters, rendering them nearly indestructible.*

The Vampire has a Fly Speed of 10 feet and can hover. The Vampire can move through other creatures and objects as if they were Difficult Terrain. It takes 5 (1d10) Force damage if it ends its turn inside an object. It also gains resistance to all damage types except Force, Psychic, and Radiant. 

**Increases CR by 2.** Granting the vampire this trait increases its CR by 2 rather than 1.

#### Vomit Blood (Vampiric Power)

*The vampire spews forth the remnants of its last meal in a crimson torrent. Those bathed in the frothing blood suffer rapid necrosis as the tainted vitae eats away at flesh.*

*Constitution Saving Throw:* each creature in a 20-foot cone.

 *Failure:*  18 (4d8) Necrotic damage. 
 *Failure by 5+:* The target can't regain hit points for 1 minute.  
 *Success:* Half damage only.

The vampire can't use this power again until it regains life from its bite attack.

\pagebreakNum

#### Walking Death (Vampiric Power)

*The vampire can fixate upon a mortal, imposing its unholy presence on the victim. The target’s mind becomes consumed by thoughts of impending death, leaving them mentally crippled by fear and despair.*

*Wisdom Saving Throw:* one creature the vampire can see within 60 feet.

 *Failure:* The creature becomes Frightened of the  Vampire until the end of the vampire’s next turn. While Frightened, the creature is Dazed* and can’t cast spells other than cantrips.  <br>
 *Failure by 5+:* While Frightened, the target is Paralyzed.  <br>
 *Success:* The creature is immune to the vampire's Walking Death for 1 hour. <br>

#### Dredge (Vampiric Power)

*With a flick of its pale hand, the vampire calls broken undead back to their unholy service. Souls bound to such a master do not escape so easily, and shattered corpses claw their way back into accursed motion.*

The vampire chooses the remains of an Undead creature of a lower CR than its own within 60 feet of it that was destroyed within the last 1 minute. That creature returns to undeath with 1 hit point.

This power can’t restore an Undead creature that is missing vital body parts, at the DM’s discretion.

#### Haemorrhage Magic (Vampiric Trait)

*The vampire can wither the spellcasting of others. With a disdainful utterance, an opposing spell destabilizes as its power bleeds away, causing the incantation to falter and its effects to weaken.*

The vampire can cast *Dampen Magic* at will. When casting the spell, it uses its Vampiric Power save DC.

**Increases CR by 2.** Granting the vampire this trait increases its CR by 2 rather than 1.

#### Lore Master (Vampiric Trait)

*The vampire has delved into forbidden mystic arts, wielding deadly spells to lay its foes low.*

The vampire chooses one of the following Lores of Magic: Beast, Death, Fire, Heavens, Metal, Shadow, or Vampiric. The chosen lore determines the traits it gains and the spells it knows. The vampire’s rank determines the number of spell slots it has and the levels of spells it can cast.

The vampire can cast its spells without material components. When casting a spell, it uses its Vampiric Power Save DC and Attack Bonus for its spell save DC and spell attack bonus. Its spellcasting ability modifier is always +5.

More powerful vampires gain access to additional spells and higher-level spell slots, as determined by their rank.
